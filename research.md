I use computational methods — natural language processing, large language models, and cultural analytics — to study inequality, bias, and norms in sociotechnical systems. My work spans AI ethics and value alignment, digital hermeneutics, and the cultural analysis of games and tourism.

## Norms and Biases in AI

_How algorithmic systems encode, enforce, and unsettle social norms, and who bears the cost._

- [Interaction Protocol Shapes Moral Judgment in Multi-Agent Debate](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.10002) <span class="venue">COLM 2026</span><span class="gloss">How the structure of a debate shapes the moral judgments language models reach</span>
- The Fabricated Front: GenAI and the Social Organization of Workplace Legibility <span class="venue">PCAIDE 2026 (forthcoming)</span><span class="gloss">How generative AI reshapes what counts as competent, legible work</span>
- [The Fragility of Moral Judgment in Large Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05651) <span class="venue">ACM FAccT 2026</span><span class="gloss">Why model moral judgments shift when the same dilemma is reframed</span>
